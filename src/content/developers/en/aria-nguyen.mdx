### Simulated Interview with Aria Nguyen

**Q1: What kind of games are you most excited to build right now?**  
I am focused on narrative-forward **adventure** experiences set in dense **open-world** spaces that reward curiosity. I love when a player can ignore the critical path for an hour and still discover a story thread, a strange character, or a hidden environmental clue that feels intentional.

**Q2: How does your role as Worldbuilding Lead shape your process?**  
As Worldbuilding Lead, I start with the emotional map of a region before I define mechanics. In practice, that means building landmarks, faction tensions, and local myths first, then designing quests that feel like natural consequences of the world rather than isolated mission prompts.

**Q3: What does your AI-assisted workflow look like day to day?**  
I use **ChatGPT** to brainstorm alternate quest outcomes and optional dialogue arcs, especially when I want to test tonal range quickly. I use **Claude** for long-form lore synthesis and consistency checks across story documents, and **Cursor** to speed up implementation passes for scripting hooks and content tooling.

**Q4: How do adventure and open-world tags influence design decisions?**  
The **adventure** tag pushes me to prioritize pacing, wonder, and narrative payoff, while the **open-world** tag forces me to design for player-driven order of discovery. Every biome, side activity, and traversal route has to carry storytelling weight, even if a player encounters it "out of sequence."

**Q5: Can you share a recent challenge and how you solved it?**  
A recent challenge was making side quests feel meaningful without overwhelming players with checklist fatigue. I solved it by grouping quests into small narrative clusters tied to place identity, then used AI-generated variants as drafts before curating them manually for emotional clarity and thematic cohesion.

**Q6: What are you aiming to learn or build next?**  
I want to build a more reactive open-world narrative layer where NPC memory changes ambient dialogue and quest entrances based on prior player behavior. My goal is to combine handcrafted story direction with AI-assisted prototyping so each adventure still feels authored but more alive.
