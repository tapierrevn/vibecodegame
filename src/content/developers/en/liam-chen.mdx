### Simulated Interview with Liam Chen

**Q1: What kind of games are you most excited to build right now?**  
I love blending **arcade** clarity with **RPG** progression and **roguelite** unpredictability. My favorite projects are ones where each run feels immediately readable but character builds and encounter outcomes still surprise experienced players.

**Q2: How does your role as Gameplay Engineer shape your workflow?**  
As Gameplay Engineer, I focus on making mechanics feel precise under pressure. I care about input timing, combat readability, and encounter cadence, then I reinforce those systems with progression logic that makes each upgrade choice meaningful.

I use **ChatGPT** to draft alternate combat rules, status-effect interactions, and reward loop ideas during concept phases. I use **Claude** to reason through complex balancing edge cases and to refine implementation plans before coding high-impact changes.

The **arcade** tag keeps controls crisp and instantly understandable, while the **RPG** tag pushes long-term build identity. The **roguelite** tag ensures failure still teaches something useful, with procedural variation that encourages adaptation rather than repetition.

**Q5: Can you share a recent challenge and solution?**  
We saw players converging on one dominant build too early in progression. I reworked item synergies, adjusted risk-reward pacing across runs, and used AI-assisted simulation prompts to identify outlier combinations before updating live balance.

**Q6: What are you aiming to build next?**  
I want to build a combat sandbox where arcade responsiveness and roguelite variety coexist with deeper RPG buildcraft. The next milestone is a smarter encounter director that reacts to player style without sacrificing fairness or readability.
