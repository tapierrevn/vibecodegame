### Simulated Interview with Maya Patel

**Q1: What kind of games are you most excited to build right now?**  
I am most excited by games that merge logical **puzzle** structures with high-tension **stealth** play. I love experiences where every room is a problem space and every solution reflects the player’s planning style.

**Q2: How does your role as Systems Designer shape your process?**  
As a Systems Designer, I focus on readable rule sets that create many valid strategies. I design guard behavior, visibility logic, tool affordances, and objective layering so players can improvise while still understanding consequence and risk.

**Q3: How do AI tools fit into your daily workflow?**  
I use **Cursor** to prototype systemic interactions and quickly test small rule changes in stealth scenarios. I use **Claude** for design reasoning, edge-case exploration, and narrative variant drafting before I curate and tighten each level’s authored intent.

**Q4: How do puzzle and stealth tags influence design decisions?**  
The **puzzle** tag means clarity is non-negotiable: players must be able to read systems and form hypotheses. The **stealth** tag requires controlled tension, predictable enemy logic, and elegant fail states that encourage adaptation rather than frustration.

**Q5: Can you share a challenge you recently solved?**  
We had a level where players felt forced into one route, which reduced both stealth creativity and puzzle satisfaction. I added alternate timing windows, tool-based bypasses, and AI-assisted test-path generation to ensure multiple viable strategies.

**Q6: What are you aiming to build next?**  
I want to build a stealth puzzle framework where mission spaces evolve in response to player behavior across chapters. The next step is stronger systemic reactivity while maintaining the clear mental models that make puzzle-driven stealth rewarding.
