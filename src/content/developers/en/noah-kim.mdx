### Simulated Interview with Noah Kim

**Q1: What kind of games are you most excited to build right now?**  
I am excited by high-speed **arcade** titles with the intensity of modern **action** combat. I want players to feel immediate control confidence, then discover deeper timing and spacing layers as they push difficulty.

**Q2: How does your role as Lead Designer shape your approach?**  
As Lead Designer, I define the core interaction loop first and protect it through every production phase. I align UX, animation timing, and level pacing around that loop so the action feels readable, punchy, and consistently rewarding.

**Q3: What does your AI-assisted workflow look like?**  
I use **ChatGPT** to generate mechanic variants, tutorial approaches, and encounter ideas during exploration phases. I use **Cursor** for rapid implementation and iteration on prototype logic, then we validate decisions with telemetry and hands-on playtesting.

**Q4: How do arcade and action tags influence your design decisions?**  
The **arcade** tag pushes us toward low friction, instant retries, and clear feedback loops. The **action** tag demands stronger enemy readability, responsive controls, and encounter escalation that tests skill rather than just reaction speed.

**Q5: Can you share a challenge and how you solved it?**  
Our mid-game difficulty ramp was too steep, causing churn before players reached advanced mechanics. I restructured encounter sequencing, improved visual telegraphs, and used AI-assisted scenario drafting to test alternate progression curves quickly.

**Q6: What are you aiming to build next?**  
I want to build an action-forward arcade platform where movement tech, combo routing, and boss scripting all reinforce one skill language. The goal is a game that feels approachable on day one and deeply expressive by week three.
