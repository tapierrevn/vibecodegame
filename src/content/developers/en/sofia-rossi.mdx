### Simulated Interview with Sofia Rossi

**Q1: What kind of games are you most excited to build right now?**  
I am most excited by gentle **simulation** games with a warm **casual** rhythm that supports daily play. I want players to feel cared for by the systems: clear goals, low pressure, and meaningful progress through small moments.

**Q2: How does your role as Product Designer influence your process?**  
As Product Designer, I design for emotional continuity across sessions. I focus on interface tone, progression pacing, and reward cadence so the simulation remains engaging without demanding intense mechanical precision from casual players.

**Q3: What does your AI-assisted workflow look like?**  
I use **Claude** to shape content frameworks, narrative micro-copy, and consistency checks for long-form progression arcs. I use **Copilot** to accelerate implementation details in UI and systems tooling, then rely on structured playtests for final tuning.

**Q4: How do simulation and casual tags influence design decisions?**  
The **simulation** tag keeps me focused on coherent world rules and long-term systems depth. The **casual** tag reminds me to reduce friction everywhere: readable onboarding, forgiving mechanics, and progression that feels steady instead of punishing.

**Q5: What challenge have you recently solved?**  
We noticed players loved early sessions but dropped once management complexity increased too quickly. I rebalanced feature unlock order, simplified decision surfaces, and used AI-assisted content pass planning to keep depth while preserving the cozy casual tone.

**Q6: What are you aiming to build next?**  
I want to build a simulation game where personalization and seasonal content keep casual players returning for months. The next milestone is a stronger live-content pipeline that scales variety without diluting clarity or emotional warmth.
