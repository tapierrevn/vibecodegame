### Simulated Interview with Zoe Park

**Q1: What kind of games are you most excited to build right now?**  
I am excited by competitive projects that combine the momentum of **racing** with the intensity of **action** game design. I want speed to be tactical, where movement choices and split-second decisions matter as much as raw reflexes.

**Q2: How does your role as Game Director shape your process?**  
As Game Director, I define a clear gameplay thesis and keep every discipline aligned to it. I spend a lot of time on decision framing: what to prototype, what to cut, and how to preserve a cohesive player identity from first lap to high-level play.

**Q3: How do you use AI in your day-to-day workflow?**  
I use **ChatGPT** for early concept exploration, mode ideation, and communication framing for cross-team alignment. I use **Gemini** to compare strategic design alternatives and evaluate system trade-offs before we commit to expensive production decisions.

**Q4: How do racing and action tags influence your design choices?**  
The **racing** tag means precision handling, readable track language, and skill-based route planning. The **action** tag pushes us toward stronger encounter tension, high-impact feedback, and more expressive risk-reward moments during high-speed play.

**Q5: Can you share a recent challenge and solution?**  
We had a prototype where spectacle was strong but competitive clarity was weak in crowded matches. I led a redesign of camera rules, collision communication, and visual priority systems, then used AI-assisted review checklists to speed up iteration across teams.

**Q6: What are you aiming to build next?**  
I want to build a racing-action experience with esports-grade readability and broad player accessibility. The next target is a mode structure that supports both quick public matches and long-term mastery for highly competitive players.
